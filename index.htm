About Me

                                   
> I am an assistant professor in the Telecom Department INSA Lyon . I am also part of the CITI Lab which does research on a board set of topics ranging from the wireless sensor networks, embedded systems to the distributed systems and data science. Before coming to INSA Lyon, I was a post-doctoral researcher in the Laborotire Hubert Curien Saint-Etienne. I received my Ph.D. degree from the Université Jean Monnet operated under Université Lyon. During my Ph.D. I was part of the Connected Intelligence team in Laboratoire Hubert Curien. I was a graduate member of University of Edinburgh and University of Trento.

> Research Interests: My primary research interests are query optimisations and the detection of complex patterns within the context of streaming processing systems. I study both how to design effective complex event processing systems as well as the relational behind extracting knowledge from the complex patterns. Consequently, my transdisciplinary work connects database optimisations, real-time data processing, Semantic Web and graph databases. In general, I develop systems that employ low-level data storage and querying optimisations to process graph-based data streams with limited memory and having a real-time response. My past work has primarily focused on how to process stateless query operators – such as selection, aggregation, filtering, top-k – over RDF graph streams. In addition, I proposed a query language and a framework to efficiently process stateful operators – such as sequencing, conjunction, disjunctions, Kleene-+, negation, etc. – over such RDF graph streams. My more recent work considers the predictive response for a complex event processing system, hence it contributes to the field of data mining.
